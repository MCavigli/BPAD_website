The best predictor of future performance is performance observed under similar conditions.

BPAD is based on the principle that the closer you put your candidate to the job they are applying for, the better you can assess their performance. Our process is designed to make candidates solve difficult situations and prove they have the skills for public safety positions. BPAD test scenes are developed with law enforcement Subject Matter Experts (SME's) and reflect the critical incidents officers will experience on the job.

BPAD has been in use for over 20 years. Numerous validation studies have demonstrated the predictive power of video testing and BPAD's effectiveness in measuring behavioral competency.

Validation research: FL State Study | Technical Report 2013

Here are 3 reasons why behavioral competency is an essential component in the public safety field:

  • Police liability research shows poor behavioral competence is the primary factor underlying liability claims and losses, including excessive use of force.
  • The majority of police activity involves human interaction, whether it is giving directions, issuing a ticket, resolving a dispute, handling a domestic disturbance, or arresting a violent offender.
  • Researchers have consistently demonstrated that behaviorally competent people are able to handle stressful situations better than those who have difficulty handling interpersonal problems.

Unbiased testing practices ensure fairness and effectiveness.
